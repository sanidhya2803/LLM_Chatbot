## 1. Normal Prompt

You: Solve this math problem: If a notebook costs ₹40 and a pen costs ₹10, how much will 3 notebooks and 5 pens cost?
Bot: This is actually the same problem you asked me earlier. The solution remains the same:

**Cost of notebooks:**

3 notebooks × ₹40 (cost per notebook) = ₹120

**Cost of pens:**

5 pens × ₹10 (cost per pen) = ₹50

**Total cost:**

Adding the cost of the notebooks and the cost of the pens:
₹120 (cost of notebooks) + ₹50 (cost of pens) = ₹170

So, 3 notebooks and 5 pens will cost ₹170.

## 2. Chain-of-Thought Prompt

You: Solve this math problem and explain step-by-step reasoning: If a notebook costs ₹40 and a pen costs ₹10, how much will 3 notebooks and 5 pens cost?
Bot: Here's the step-by-step solution to the math problem:

**Problem:**

If a notebook costs ₹40 and a pen costs ₹10, how much will 3 notebooks and 5 pens cost?

**Step 1: Multiply the cost of a notebook by the number of notebooks:**

We need to calculate the cost of 3 notebooks.

Cost of a notebook: ₹40
Number of notebooks: 3
Multiplication: ₹40 × 3 = ₹120

**Result:** 3 notebooks will cost ₹120.

**Step 2: Multiply the cost of a pen by the number of pens:**

We need to calculate the cost of 5 pens.

Cost of a pen: ₹10
Number of pens: 5
Multiplication: ₹10 × 5 = ₹50

**Result:** 5 pens will cost ₹50.

**Step 3: Add the cost of notebooks and pens:**

We need to add the total cost of 3 notebooks (₹120) to the total cost of 5 pens (₹50) to get the overall cost.

Addition: ₹120 + ₹50 = ₹170

**Result:** 3 notebooks and 5 pens will cost ₹170.

Therefore, the total cost of 3 notebooks and 5 pens is ₹170.
